Star Wars 501st Helmet

See Helmet Making Process under References for more in-depth details on putting it together and finishing it.

File Used: Clone Trooper Helmet Phase 2 Star Wars

I didn't use the creator's original parts for some reason... I used Meshmixer to cut it into 16 parts that fit my printer better. In total it probably used up about 1 kg of filament, which isn't that bad in my opinion. I'd rather spend $20 on filament and have the special connection of making it, rather than buying one for $120.

June 30, 2020

Tie Fighter

File Used: Tie Fighter

This was even before the Kenobi lightsaber. This was partly a test print a bit after I had gotten my printer, so obviously if only half it was used... something went wrong.

I was still learning the basics of Cura and was copying someone's settings... when I mistyped the infill density to be 80 instead of 8.0. That is why some of the Kenobi parts and this part as well are very fragile. Along with that, I didn't understand the idea of, letting a print cool down, so when taking it off with the scraper, the tips of the ends warped up. Therefore, it wouldn't make a flush fit. I thought about how to fix it, but I didn't think it was worth the effort. I was oringially going to scrap it but had an idea to just use half of it and put command strips on the back and put it on the wall next to my other displays. I may later add an X-Wing or Y-Wing to complete the vibe.
